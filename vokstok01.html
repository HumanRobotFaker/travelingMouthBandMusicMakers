In the mines, there is only cold and darkness. The companymen say any energy transformation from electric to radiant simultaneously generates the thermal variety. The ice must stay cold. After all, that's what the people pay for.

     Sometimes I daydream of other places we go. Sri Lanka. Davao City. Dakar.

     But here I am. Vostok.

     We chisel three-dimensional shapes and remove them by feel. We slide them down the frictionless™ chute and step forward to chisel the next cube.

     They say the cave is sturdy. But I've heard of mine collapses.

     I know I've just begun, but I'm already daydreaming of when my shift ends in fifteen hours. Sitting in my domicile, my BlikBlok™ headset enveloping over my corporeal sensory inputs, as thought-texts manifest to and from my friends who live across the globe.

     But not now.

     Fifteen hours to go.
