     “There are dreams before lucidity and dreams after lucidity,” Hector announced to the crowd of eight, as they drank their yeefass. “Before lucidity, dreams are a chaos of narrative tension: nonsensical imperatives of great import, anxiety about being unable to fulfill s’posed requirements, and a sea of characters whose very faces and personalities shift and transform at any given moment.”

     He paused.

     “After lucidity is something else altogether”

     “Tell us more,” Andy said.

     But Hector would not.

:: .15 ::

     The parties continued every night. It was summer, after all. Even when no bonfire was planned, a fire, in some form or fashion, was cultivated. Andy kept a metal barrell into the back of his pickup truck, just in case there was no other suitable spot to cultivate the flames.

     The night of the first barrell fire was when Jakob and Luuk declared Andy to be the «Bellipotentate of Lithomania».

     “Lead us, oh great Potentate!” Jakob called.

     Luuk placed atop Andy’s head a “crown of stone” (styrofoam balls painted grey). “Lead us, oh great Potentate!” Luuk said.

     So Andrew led them back to Hector’s house.
