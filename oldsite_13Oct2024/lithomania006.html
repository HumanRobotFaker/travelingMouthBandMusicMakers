     The rocks were laid atop a blue tarp in the center of Hector's backyard. A whole team was assembled. Andrew and Jakob received the rocks, whilst Luuk and Hector loaded the rocks. The cyclists were all sorts of people: Luuk’s party friends, Jakob’s wrestling buddies, and a number of girls with whom Andrew ran cross-country.

     Luuk brought «lunatic lichens» for [him] and Hector to chew. This made rock excavation more interesting.

     “Wow, this rock is so smooth,” Luuk said, rubbing it with his finger before passing it to Hector.

     “Yeah,” Hector said, barely able to concentrate on anything but the one task at hand.

     As the rocks piled atop the blue tarp, Andrew and Jakob began constructing a 25-square meter area using tape measures, stakes, and string. Then they began scraping the spongy topsoil on the perimeter to find a sturdier base upon which they began piling the rocks.
