     Thanks to the ubiquity of municipally-operated wikiCameras, the prevalence of AV still+move frames made it more than possible for machineLearning softward to create photos and videos of anyone alive doing absolutely anything at all. Thus, when my father received a mailed envelope of my mother performing indecent acts with a man in our front yard, his first instinct was that this was a product of psychological warfare performed by a foreign gov't (or acteur). After all, my father developed autonomous AI self-repair systems for solar-powered drones so they could stay permanently in flight. And, thus, consequently, it might be a worthwhile pursuit to throw a stick into the spokes of his mental bicycle.

     But something about the absurdity of it - the front yard rather than a nondescript hotel room - made him question the nonveracity of it. So he showed the photos to mother and she immediately broke down in confession.

     My parents, 'mazda bless 'em, didn't totally self-destruct in the aftermath. The only reason that I even knew it happened was that - for a hot moment - I was a snoop, a spy, a radical provocateur in favour of the absolute unveiling of all secrets that lay in the shadows. A force accelerating culture toward The Total Trust Society. But then, suddenly burrowed underneath the surface of the parental-provided narrative of our family's histoire, I faced the decision of what to do with that information. Put it on a billboard? Confront my parents? And in the end, I did what most common people probably do: pretend I knew nothing new and did nothing new except look at my parents' relationship through a new lens.

     Except I told Hector. Because Hector was my transectional TTS™ buddy at the time. Albeit, I told him not to tell anyone.

     "Doesn't that violate the whole ideology of TTS™?" he asked. "Us keeping secrets from others?"

     Hector, in the past, had already aired all his parents' dirty laundry.

     "Maybe we're confused," I said. "I mean, the solshine doesn't radiate upon every square meter of terra perpetually. There's a time for illumination and there's an equally long period of darkness."

     He smirked. "On average. It's not equal right now. And the ratio changes on the daily."

     I rolled my eyes. "Okay, but you get the point."

     "That you're backpedaling?" he said. "When did this all happen?"

     "When my brother and I were real little. Toddlers."

     "And your dad forgave her?"

     I shrugged. "All I know is what was on the old laptop [I found shoved in my dad's closet]." Then I explained how my dad had deleted the files, but I'd used an AI Archaeologist specifically trained to target «deleted» files. "There's a journal entry on a text file where he basically 'xplained everything. The guy was some old boyfriend of hers that happened to be in the near proximity for work. I know there's more to the story, but all I got is a deleted [text-message exhchange] conversation he had with a friend where he says that his father gave him some advice for relationship-repair and they seemed to be working things out.
