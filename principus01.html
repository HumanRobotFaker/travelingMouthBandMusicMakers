“Maybe I made the wrong decision,” [principus] said.

     “What do you mean?” [his wife] said.

     “Maybe it wasn’t the Almighty & Awesome Ahuramazda [AAA] telling me to take this position,” he said. “Maybe the voice I heard nudging me was my own ego. My own pride. My selfish want. Or maybe worse: maybe it was Ahriman Themself.”

     “I don’t think so,” she said. “We communed long and hard about it.”

     “Maybe we were both drawn to something less pure. Maybe it was the material considerations that ultimately swayed us: the better health insurance and the higher pay. Maybe I was just fatigued and worn out by all the disciplinary methods I had to utilize. Feeling unable to do what was necessary to actually transform Higgins without substantial struggle, so I took the easier path. The path more traveled. The climb up the ladder of power. The attempt to be a «big man».”

     He was clearly in one of those moods. Growing up poor and with a distracted father, he had developed an ideology as a youth that he couldn’t quite let go of: the idea that the rich and successful were actually agents of Ahriman. He couldn’t quite believe that [AAA] would want him to model to the downtrodden youth that a black man could rise to a professional status of high acclaim. He couldn’t quite believe that [AAA] would want that for him.

     And she was tired of telling that to him. So she sipped her freshly iced, tea-infused water.

     “Well, you know what I think,” she said.

     “These kids in BeaverCreek,” he said, “they don’t need me.”

     “Don’t let their average [SES] fool you; everybody needs somebody. And don’t give me that rot that they’re all well-off, because I know better.”

     He paused and looked at the ground. “Somebody contacted me about a new position. Near Cincinnati. Better pay and better… everything,.”

     She nodded. “How much would it affect your commute?”

     “About fifteen minutes more. Each way.”

     “Something to consider in your calculations.”

     “But doesn’t it feel ridiculous? I’ve only been at BeaverCreek a single year?”

     She shrugged her shoulders. “Have you been communing with [AAA]?”

     “I’ve been trying.”

     She set down her glass and leaned forward. “I don’t believe you are meant to be stuck at any particular provincial backwater. I believe you are a man who is destined to rise to a position where you can make vast, great, sweeping changes in how we educate the children of these federated states. And – as we’ve discussed in the past – it seems that an important aspect is you getting a sense of how many different skools operate. From Higgins to the highest paying skools in the state.”

     He nodded.

     “It’s about being better able to heal this broken world.”
