XIII.

     MarsBar stopped by KingLeroy’s mansion to check Hector’s vitals, roll him to avoid bedsores, and empty his piss canisters. His 3-ring binder elucidating his subcerebral adventures grew to novella length. Naturally, like a creepy male cur, I stalked her.

     “You know Hector’s mom’s gonna flip when she comes home and sees what you’ve done to her house,” MarsBar said.

     “We haven’t messed with any of her stuff,” I said. “It’s all still here. And nobody’s been in her bedroom. We literally screwed in that giant plywood portrait of King Leroy [over her door] to ensure that.”

     “Y’all’re delusional. She’s gonna call the cops. Especially when she finds her son drugged unconscious in a glass casket.”

     “The police? Nah. Hector told us she’s a prostitute. Prostitutes don’t call the police.”

     “He said she was like a prostitute; she’s not an actual prostitute, hooking on the street, addicted to the guat’, working for a pimp. She meets up with men from the interwebs, gets them invested enough that they’ll lend her monies or help pay bills, and possibly/probably has sex with them. There’s a difference.”

     “Is there?” I asked.

     “There is. One’s not afraid to call the police on all y’all fools.”

     “But Hector says she cheats the gov’t out of disability benefits.”

     “She’s not running some scam, where she’s a doctor pretending to have eighty disability patients they ghost-treat,” MarsBar said. “She’s a patient, one with legit medical records, and self-reported ailments that’re probably [animal Faeces], but which’re difficult to prove as outright fraudulent. She’s not afraid to call the local police, keyword being local not federal.”

     “How do you know all this?”

     “Because for a time, I was interested in uncovering the enigma that is Hector David Chavez. And if you don’t think one’s features and foibles stem from the family, you’re fooling yourself.”
