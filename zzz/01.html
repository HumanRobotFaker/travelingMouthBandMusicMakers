I.

     Hector hung out with me the day before he inserted himself into the glass coffin, an act that has widely been criticized as being “self-indulgent and derivative”. His demeanor could be described as manic and enthused. Oh what great wonders and wisdom he’d encounter upon his medically-induced multi-week foray into the LucidDreamRealmTM! Nevermind (and certainly not discuss) the aspect that this motivation toward self-obliteration – temporary as it may be – was precipitated by the break-up of his relationship with Britni Lyn Sheba. Ahuramazda had destined this emulation of Dr. Dalton so Hector could ascend to TheNextLevel of his transformation into the UltimateProtagonist, long heralded and sung about on the [10 000] pages of the Divine Anthology.

     “My avatar is going to lead me to the ultimate ground of being within the black hole at the center of our spiral galaxy,” Hector declared, as he cleaned up piles of dirty clothes littered across his bedroom floor.

    “You know what actually happens when you encounter a black hole?” Winfield asked. “Spaghetti-fication.”

     Hector steamed forward as if Winfield said nothing. “Just as chemical reactions are the deconstruction of molecular bonds, followed by their reconstruction albeit in different formations, so shalt I be. I shall emerge constituted in different patterns that yield different substances featuring different characteristic properties. Different magnitudes of potential bond energy! Alas, I will be a butterfly emerging from a chrysalis!”

    “You’re mixing metaphors,” Winfield said. “And maybe you’ll emerge as a tiger moth, with no mouth, doomed to no purpose but to flutter around and reproduce before you inevitably starve to death.”

     Hector turned slowly and smiled at Winfield. “Biology is so inspirational. I mean, when there’s a will, there’s a way; am I right?”

     “Wrong,” Winfield said. “But inspiration, as in cellular respiration, the blind diffusion of oxygen molecules that eventually bounce their way to an alien mitochondrion that shits out ATP molecules, is a pretty interesting idea.”

     “Interest. Collecting interest. Compound interest.”

     “Y’all,” Uriah Bleu said. “I can’t keep up.”

     Winfield turned to Uriah and smiled.

     “That’s because you’re from Alabama, a re-tester, and should be incarcerated with all the other hypointels at the BeaverCreek EduCation Center. Plus, you’re high on all the guatama flower buds that [Andrew] Ramblehurst loaded you with.”

     Uriah grinned. “I’d like to inform all the folks streaming at home that GFBs are illegal due to their possession of a particular isomer of the TNT molecular compound. These [GFBs] which I’ve consumed are a different isomer, which have – as of this date – not been explicitly declared illicit in any form whatsoever by these here federated states.”

     Winfield thought-texted everyone a GIF of the TNT molecule (stick-and-ball model), with one of its double-bonds detached and swapping locales with a single bond part of the same ring. “Now that Uriah’s legal defense rests, I want to declare the possibility that I may eschew all ethical considerations and attempt to poison Hector while he lays himself in the Dr. Dalton 3000 LucidDreamGlassCasket. I’m thinking I could contaminate his medical IV bag with hungryHungryHeterotrophs.”

     “Is that a threat?” Hector asked.

     “No, just a possibility. PastMe could never have predicted what CurrentMe is; why would CurrentMe be able to predict where FutureMe is?”

     “What about CurrentYou was obfuscated from the imagination of PastYou?” I asked.

     “That I’d be a cyborg who [fooled] around all day and received prizes from NyleDroneBots as positive operant conditioning for allowing AutomaticAlgorithmicAdvisor apps to skew my daily behavior as some milestone of peak academic achievement.”

     And then everyone roared with laughter so loud that Yeshi temporarily appeared inside Hector’s [death] shrine.
