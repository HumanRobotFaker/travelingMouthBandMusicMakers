higher hire

     The speed of light is woefully inadequate for interstellar travel, but just perfect for intraterrestial nigh-instantaneous communication. Who knew where the executives of TMB University were located on the globe? The local kids joked it was on a spaceship nestled at the bottom of the ocean at Oo latitude and Oo longitude, but that didn’t make a whole lot of sense. Nonetheless, when Mister Allen put on the SpaceHelmet™, he was instantaneously on a Himalayan mountain being interviewed by a minion. “You’re hired,” the minion said.

     “I’ll tender my resignation with my current employer,” Mister Allen said.

     “Did you have an issue with the terms of service?”

     “Not that I can see.”

     “Excellent. Well, to start, you’ll be interviewed by one of your students.”

     From a nearby cave entrance, a young [hispanic] boy emerged. He held a microphone. When Mister Allen swiveled his head to look at the minion, he found it gone.

     The boy had an old-fashioned cassette tape recorder in hand. He loaded a cassette and pointed the microphone at Mister Allen.

     “How do you morally feel about the installation of nanochips into the brains of penguins?”

     “What?”

     “For the purposes of increasing communication between the penguins. Do you have any aPriori objection to such meddling with nature?”

     Mister Allen swallowed. “For what purpose?”

     “The purpose matters?”

     Mister Allen smirked. “The purpose always matters.”

     The young boy scrawled some marks in his hand-sized notebook.

     “Well,” the boy finally said, pointing the microphone back at Mister Allen. “There are disagreements about the true purpose. Disagreements abut what the sincere purpose is.”

     Mister Allen nodded. The cirrus clouds looked so real. “What do you believe the real purpose is? For implanting nanochips into penguin brains?”

     “I think it is a confluence of factors.”

     Mister Allen laughed. “A confluence, huh?”

     “Money, global security, a scientific inquiry into the nature of consciousness itself…”

     “How money?”

     “The development of an adept and obedience workforce whose whole cost is krill and upkeep.”

     “Upkeep.”

     “And krill.”

     “Well,” Mister Allen said. “I don’t know about all this. But I’ll say this :- folks’ notion of natural is skewed. Everything humans do is natural. If beavers building dams is natural and bird nests are natural, then all human tool-making and meddling is natural.”

    

     The next day, Mister Allen showed up to the office at the [empty space] building on the corner of Dayton-Xenia ST and Grange Hall RD in BeaverCreek OIHO to find a veritable parade occurring. Twenty teenagers pedaled floats on modified bicycles, waving bright ornj flags emblazoned with the Antarctica symbol, and tooting horns.

     “Welcome! Welcome!” the children sang.

     “Thank you,” Mister Allen said. “Thank you.”

     The kids looked at Mister Allen quizzically. “Welcome to the new embassy for the Kingdom of Antarctica.”

     Mister Allen watched the children lower the flag for these here federated states and replace it with the Antarctica flag.

     “Ummm…” Mister Allen said.
