Xiander didn’t want to do anything. Nothing. Nada.

     He once wanted to do things. He once felt inspired to take part in activities. But no longer.

     He just wanted to lay in bed.

     For three days, he didn’t put in his iContacts™. On day four, the doorbell started ringing.

     “Xiander,” his mother sang, “some classmates are at the door.”

     Xiander dragged himself to the front door.

     “Boiled yeshi,” Mars said. “You look like kur.”

     “I’ve been sick,” Xiander said.

     “You didn’t report as sick. There’s no biomedical indication that you’re sick.”

     Xiander shrugged.

     “Are you depressed? Lost in self-absorption?” Mars asked. “Or are you just hitting the Roxxxie™ too hard?”

     “Shut up.”

     “What? These are reasonable questions. You remember what happened to Winfield, don't you?”

     “Oh, so I’m Winfield?”

     Mars shrugged. “First, self-absorbed depression; second, anti-natalist, nihilistic destruction of communal neighbors.”

     “By not participating in skool?”

     “Our rankings are dropping. And the Triple A’s blame you.”

     “Cry me a river,” and Xiander slammed the door on Mars.
