Photosynthesis is the process by which green plants and some other organisms use sunlight to synthesize foods with the help of chlorophyll. It is an essential part of the carbon cycle and is one of the most important biochemical pathways.

Mitosis is the process by which a cell divides its nucleus and contents to produce two new cells, each with the same amount of DNA as the original cell. It is a type of cell division that occurs in somatic cells and is essential for growth and repair of tissues.

A magnetic field is the area around a magnet where magnetic force can be detected. It is a vector field that describes the magnetic influence of electric currents and magnetized materials.
