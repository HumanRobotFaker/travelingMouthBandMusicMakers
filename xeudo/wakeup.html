WakeUp


"Nightmare?" Winfield says as you shoot up. He sits on an office chair, another cigarette pissing into the air.

"Yeah."

"You kept calling out for your mom."

"Oh, was I?" you say. "Where is everybody?"

"They went home."

"Oh. Is this your house?"

Winfield drags on the cigarette, the cherry brightening intensely. "Nope," he says in a cloud of smoke. "This is Hector's house."

"Hector? Where's he?"

"He's on vacation," Winfield says. "I'm house-sitting."

"Oh okay," you say.

"Let me ask you this:— Do you believe in Ahuramazda, the all-good, all-knowing, all-powerful deity, ultimate ground of being, oo-GOB?""

"What? Why?" you ask.

Winfield smirks. "That affects your answer?"

"I mean, sure it does, are you going to cut off my head if I answer wrong?"

Winfield laughs. "I'm just trying to see who I'm dealing with. So I figure we cut to the important business."
